Shoebill

This page is all about the Shoebill (Balaeniceps rex)

Intro

The Shoebill or the Balaeniceps rex is known by many names, including whalebird, whale-headed-stork and shoe-billed stork. It is a large bird with long-legs. Thogh it looks more like a stork, genetically it is placed with peilcans and herons. Adults are grey and juveniles are typically more brown. The Shoebill lives in tropical east Africa in large swamps.

Diet

Shoebills are mainly piscivorous, meaning that they are carnivores that mainly eat fish. They also prey a large amount of wetland vertebrates. Given their sharp-edged beak, wide beak gape, and size they tedn to hunt larger animals. Some specific fish they prey on are...

  • lungfish
  • Tilapia
  • Catfish
  • water snakes
  • frogs

Voice

The Shoebill is normally silent, but their bill can create a lot of clattering noise. They tend to utter like a cow's moo. They have also been heard making high-pitched whines.

habitat

The Shoebill is distributed in freshwater swamps of central tropical Africa. The species is found is Sudan, Congo, Rwanda, Uganda, and Zambia. They tend to be foudn where ther is an abundance of lungfish and other fish. They are also foudn in deep water reagions.
